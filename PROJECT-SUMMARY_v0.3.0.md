# Advanced Design Certificate in AI-Augmented Cultural Systems & Experiences

## Overview

This is a **nine-microcredential (MC) certificate program** — an educational curriculum designed to teach competencies in cultural production, organizational design, and AI-augmented systems orchestration. It is structured as an Obsidian vault with Git version control.

**Version:** 0.3.0  
**Status:** In Development

---

## Structure — Three Stacks (Dialectical Arc)

The program progresses through three developmental logics aligned with a thesis–antithesis–synthesis structure:

| Stack | Developmental Logic | Dialectical Role | MCs | Status |
|-------|--------------------|------------------|-----|--------|
| **Stack 1** | **Identity Narrative Logic** | Thesis — Cultural self-articulation and meaning-making | MC1, MC2, MC3 | Fully developed |
| **Stack 2** | **Resource Aggregation Logic** | Antithesis — Organizational infrastructure, constraint, and capacity | MC4, MC5, MC6 | Outline/skeleton only |
| **Stack 3** | **Attention Orchestration Logic** | Synthesis — Coordinating identity and resources within shared attention environments | MC7, MC8, MC9 | MC7 in development; MC8–MC9 outlines |

The curriculum begins with identity formation, moves into structural resource analysis, and culminates in the orchestration of shared attention across AI-mediated ecosystems.

---

## The Three Logics Framework

The certificate is intentionally structured around three interdependent logics:

- **Identity Narrative Logic**  
  Students examine how voice, persona, and meaning are constructed and circulated within contemporary attention economies. This stack foregrounds cultural production and the lived tensions between authenticity and commodification.

- **Resource Aggregation Logic**  
  The focus shifts to organizational infrastructures, platforms, capital systems, and institutional constraints that shape cultural production. Students analyze how resources enable, limit, and condition scale and sustainability.

- **Attention Orchestration Logic**  
  The program culminates in designing shared attention environments. Students learn to coordinate identity and resources within AI-augmented ecosystems—aligning stakeholders, sponsors, communities, and governance structures responsibly through boundary objects and structured intervention.

Together, these three logics move learners from personal expression to structural analysis to strategic orchestration within complex cultural systems.
