# Advanced Design Certificate — Strategyzer Integration Overview  
**MC1–MC9 Ecosystem Summary & Partnership Positioning**

---

## Executive Overview

The Advanced Design Certificate is a nine-microcredential program that develops shared-value ecosystem designers capable of orchestrating cultural production, institutional alignment, sponsorship ecosystems, and AI-augmented governance.

The certificate integrates:

- Christensen’s Resources–Processes–Values (RPV) framework  
- Wartofsky’s three artifact levels (Primary / Secondary / Tertiary)  
- IDEO’s feasibility–viability–desirability logic (recalibrated through RPV)  
- AI governance design  
- Strategyzer Business Model & Value Proposition tools  

The program progresses intentionally:

- **Stack 1 — Processes**: Production capability  
- **Stack 2 — Resources**: Institutional literacy  
- **Stack 3 — Shared Values**: Ecosystem orchestration  

Strategyzer tools are introduced early, expanded structurally in Stack 2, and become central in Stack 3 — culminating in a capstone pitch articulated through Business Model Canvas and Value Proposition Canvas frameworks.

---

# Stack 1 — Processes  
**MC1–MC3 | Cultural Production & Narrative Framing**

### Focus  
Students develop disciplined production capability:

- Audio and narrative creation  
- Archival media engagement  
- Persona construction  
- Community-of-practice analysis  

Students learn how media artifacts shape communities and value perception.

### Strategyzer Integration  

- Introductory exposure to Business Model Canvas (BMC)
- Introductory exposure to Value Proposition Canvas (VPC)

At this stage, Strategyzer tools function as structural literacy devices — students begin understanding how value must be articulated clearly across stakeholders.

---

# Stack 2 — Resources  
**MC4–MC6 | Institutional Literacy & System Diagnosis**

### Focus  
Students develop institutional awareness and constraint literacy:

- Mapping activity systems  
- Identifying contradictions  
- Modeling implementation feasibility  
- Previsualizing installation scenarios  
- Analyzing governance structures  

Students learn that value creation operates within institutional boundaries.

### Strategyzer Integration  

- Structured use of BMC symmetry (Resources ↔ Activities ↔ Partners)  
- Feasibility/viability/desirability alignment modeling  
- Early shared-value articulation logic  

Strategyzer tools become system-mapping instruments for diagnosing institutional alignment.

---

# Stack 3 — Shared Values  
**MC7–MC9 | AI-Augmented Orchestration & Governance**

Stack 3 transforms structural literacy into ecosystem design capability.

Students architect:

- An AI-augmented branding and activation studio  
- Sponsor and donor alignment models  
- Transparent licensing and revenue flows  
- Governance-aware AI integration  
- Shared-value fundraising architecture  

Strategyzer becomes the primary articulation framework.

---

## MC7 — Studio Architecture

Students build:

- Full Business Model Canvas  
- Dual Value Proposition Canvases (Cause + Sponsor)  
- Revenue and cost structures  
- Division of labour architecture  
- AI governance boundaries  

Strategyzer tools become the language of orchestration.

Students use BMC and VPC not as worksheets, but as system alignment instruments.

---

## MC8 — Licensing & Transparency Layer

Students integrate:

- Intellectual property modeling  
- Partnership contracts  
- Revenue traceability  
- AI oversight structures  
- Transparency architecture  

Strategyzer tools clarify:

- Partnership logic  
- Revenue dependencies  
- Risk surfaces  
- Shared-value articulation  

Licensing becomes a design layer, not an afterthought.

---

## MC9 — Capstone Pitch & Ecosystem Dialogue

Students present a shared-value activation system (fundraising boundary object) using:

- Business Model Canvas articulation  
- Dual Value Proposition logic  
- Revenue realism

Strategyzer tools become the public-facing articulation interface.

Students are not required to use academic terminology (e.g., activity theory).  

Instead, they present:

A coherent, Strategyzer-articulated shared-value ecosystem design.
