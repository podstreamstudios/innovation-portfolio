Program Overview

A Dialectical Approach to Cultural Systems Education

This certificate program traces a Hegelian dialectical arc across three stacks of microcredentials. Students begin with local cultural production (thesis), encounter institutional and platform-scale tensions (antithesis), and resolve these through AI-augmented systems design and a live capstone intervention (synthesis).

The program is designed to be additive, not competitive with existing degree programs—it feeds senior undergraduate and graduate courses by formalizing competencies that are typically assumed but never formally taught.

Completion certifies foundational literacy in cultural production, institutional mediation, platform and workflow economics, multimodal experience design, and AI-augmented systems design—competencies typically assumed but not formally taught in senior undergraduate and graduate courses in arts, culture, creative industries, and design entrepreneurship.

MC1

Production Literacy: Persona, Voice, and Cultural Identity

YOU® Audio Essay / Proto-Podcast
Developed

Students construct identity as a cultural object, using voice as a medium of authority and authenticity. Operating within a pre-2007 "enhanced podcast" frame, the course examines how cultural identity is produced, performed, and mediated—then contrasts this with contemporary multi-platform persona construction via a Chris Hayes case study.

MC2

Experience Design Awareness: Cultural Systems, Icons, and Experiential Design

Curated Event / Installation / Virtual Environment
Developed

Teams apply the ARIES methodology (Autopoietic Remix for Intertextual Ecological Systems) to design immersive cultural experiences. Using pre-visualization as tertiary artifacts that shape design thinking, students transform generic ballroom spaces through cultural overlay—drawing on intertextual reservoirs, experiential assembly, and value objectification.

ARIES methodology Experiential design Intertextuality (Eco) Wartofsky artifact levels Pre-visualization Revenue modeling
Credits: 3.0 CEUs Format: Team-based, cohort Duration: 6–7 weeks Platform: Miro, Google Workspace
MC3

Platform and Ecosystem Economics: Cultural Product Tour Strategy

Multi-City Tour Design via Strategyzer
Developed

Breaking from the pre-platform time capsule of MC1–MC2, students confront the post-platform present. Assuming their MC1 cultural product achieved modest success, they design a multi-city tour strategy using Strategyzer’s Business Model Canvas, Value Proposition Canvas, and Lean Canvas—integrating MC2’s ARIES methodology for a pilot event.

Strategyzer (BMC / VPC / Lean) Tour logistics & budgeting City-tier analysis Platform economics Cultural entrepreneurship ARIES integration
Credits: 3.0 CEUs Format: Team-based, cohort Duration: 6–7 weeks Platform: Strategyzer, Google Workspace

Theoretical Foundations

Key Frameworks

Activity Theory

Modeling activity systems and identifying structural contradictions at primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary levels. Core theoretical spine from MC3 through MC9.

Change Laboratory

Strategyzer tools serve as the "second stimulus" in Change Laboratory methodology. Students design formative interventions rather than solving isolated problems.

Boundary Object Theory

The capstone fundraising event functions as a shared artifact coordinating activity systems with different institutional logics—a cause-driven organization and a sponsor ecosystem.

Wartofsky Artifact Levels

Primary (resources), secondary (processes), tertiary (perspectives). Pre-visualization tools in MC2 are treated as tertiary artifacts that shape design thinking before decisions are made.

ARIES Methodology

Autopoietic Remix for Intertextual Ecological Systems. Three layers: Cultural Reservoir (intertextual inputs), Experiential Assembly (remix operations), and Value Objectification (authorization).

Strategyzer Toolset

Business Model Canvas, Value Proposition Canvas, and Lean Canvas applied to cultural production contexts. Central to MC3, MC7, and the MC9 capstone.
