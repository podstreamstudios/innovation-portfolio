# Stack 2 — Institutional & Organizational Resources

Stack 1 developed methodological competence:

- Media construction
- Persona analysis
- Narrative framing
- Tool awareness

Stack 2 shifts focus from individual production to institutional systems.

If Stack 1 asked:

> How do we construct and frame cultural artifacts?

Stack 2 asks:

> Within what institutional infrastructures do those artifacts circulate?

This stack centers Resources — not as static assets, but as structured access within institutional ecosystems.

---

## From Individual Voice to Institutional Context

Stack 1 foregrounded persona and media construction.

Stack 2 introduces institutional mediation.

Students move from:

- Individual authorship
- Expressive framing
- Media skill development

to:

- Archival access
- Curatorial constraints
- Regulatory context
- Funding structures
- Cross-national memory politics
- Organizational mission alignment

Creative production is never free-floating.

It is embedded in structured environments.

---

## Resources as Structured Access

In RPV terms, Stack 2 focuses on:

- What resources exist?
- Who controls them?
- Who may access them?
- Under what rules?
- Within which communities of practice?

Resources include:

- Archival materials
- Institutional legitimacy
- Venue space
- Exhibition infrastructure
- National narratives
- Donor expectations
- Technological installations

Access to resources is governed by rules of engagement and exchange.

Institutional design shapes what can be produced — and what cannot.

---

## Institutional Mediation & Contradiction Literacy

Stack 2 introduces contradiction literacy.

Students learn to identify tensions between:

- Engagement vs gravitas
- Donor influence vs curatorial neutrality
- Immersive technology vs archival fidelity
- National narrative vs transnational history
- Educational mission vs spectacle

These tensions are not failures.

They are structural features of institutional life.

Through dual mapping exercises (e.g., Canadian vs U.S. WWII museums), students diagnose:

- Rules of engagement (WHERE)
- Communities of practice (WHAT)
- Division of labour (WHO)

Stack 2 strengthens structural awareness before students attempt orchestration in Stack 3.

---

## Visualization as Institutional Negotiation

In Stack 2, visualization tools are not aesthetic devices.

They are institutional negotiation instruments.

Students use previsualization to:

- Reveal potential tensions before implementation
- Model how installations alter visitor activity systems
- Anticipate regulatory friction
- Surface cross-system contradictions

Previsualization becomes a second stimulus — a way of seeing institutional constraints before material intervention.

This is structural rehearsal, not spectacle.

---

## Comparative Case Study Logic

Stack 2 mirrors Stack 1 structurally:

- Compare/contrast media practitioners (Curtis vs Crowe)
- Compare/contrast institutional contexts (Canada vs U.S.)
- Diagnose how community and institutional rules shape production

The shift is from persona to platform.

From voice to infrastructure.
