### Current Problem:

### **Preparation for MC3: Tour Strategy**

**Hayes's live events = tour model for MC3:**

**What Hayes does:**
- Records podcast episodes live at venues (The Strand bookstore, theaters, universities)
- Combines book promotion with podcast content creation
- Coordinates venue partnerships, ticket sales, guest logistics
- Multi-platform amplification (live event → podcast episode → social media clips)

**What you'll learn in MC3:**
- **Business Model Canvas:** Who creates/receives value in Hayes's tour model?
  - Audience gets live experience
  - Venue gets programming + audience
  - Hayes gets content + book sales + deeper fan engagement
  - MSNBC benefits from Hayes's increased visibility
  
- **Value Proposition Canvas:** What pains/gains does live event address?
  - **Pain:** Podcast is disembodied (can't see/meet Hayes)
  - **Gain:** Live creates embodied connection, community formation
  
- **Lean Canvas:** How does Hayes validate demand before booking venues?
  - Audience concentration (where do listeners live?)
  - Venue partnerships (bookstores want to host?)
  - Ticket pricing (what's sustainable?)

**Tour logistics you'll design in MC3:**
- City selection (NYC, LA, SF, Portland, Austin—where's demand?)
- Venue coordination (bookstores, theaters, universities)
- Multi-city timeline (6-8 weeks, 5-7 cities, sustainable pacing)
- Budget modeling (travel, venues, marketing vs. ticket + book sales)

**Your MC1 audio essay → MC3 tour strategy:**
- If your MC1 podcast gained audience → book deal → tour opportunity
- MC3 = designing the business model, logistics, validation strategy
- Hayes live events = proof that podcast-to-tour model works

**Key insight for MC1:**
When creating your audio essay, consider: "If this succeeded and I could tour with it, what would that look like?" Hayes built multi-platform presence intentionally—podcast supports TV, TV amplifies podcast, book creates tour opportunities, tours generate content. This is strategic, not accidental.

**NOTE FOR SOUNDTRAP USERS:**

Enhanced podcasts (2005-2007) required iTunes + iPod + computer syncing. **Soundtrap cannot create enhanced podcasts** because:
- Soundtrap is cloud-based (exports MP3/WAV, not .m4a enhanced format)
- Enhanced podcasts are obsolete (no modern app supports chapter markers/images fully)
- You're studying enhanced podcasts historically, not creating them

**Your MC1 audio essay:**
- Created in Soundtrap (cloud-based, collaborative)
- Exported as standard audio (MP3 suitable for podcast upload or standalone listening)
- Could be uploaded to podcast platforms (Spotify, Apple Podcasts) as regular episode

**The pedagogical point:**
- Enhanced podcasts = bounded, offline-first, multimedia (pre-smartphone era)
- Soundtrap = cloud-based, collaborative, streaming (current era)
- You create in current tools while studying historical context to understand what changed

## RECOMMENDED CHANGE 7: Update Student Questions FAQ

### Addition Needed:

**Location: Common Student Questions (Line ~749)**

**Add:**

**Q: "I'm using Soundtrap for MC1. Why are we studying GarageBand-era enhanced podcasts?"**  
A: You're not studying GarageBand specifically—you're studying PRE-PLATFORM media logic (before smartphones, algorithms, streaming). Enhanced podcasts (2005-2007) represent this era. Soundtrap is your TOOL, but you're learning CONTEXT of how media worked before cloud-based creation became possible. This historical awareness helps you make intentional choices about platform strategy in MC3.

**Q: "Will my MC1 audio essay actually become a tour in MC3?"**  
A: In MC3, you'll design a HYPOTHETICAL tour strategy for a cultural product. It could be based on your MC1 work, or a different product. The point is learning tour logistics, business modeling (Strategyzer tools), and value creation—not actually touring. Though if your MC1 work is strong and you want to design tour for it, absolutely do that!
