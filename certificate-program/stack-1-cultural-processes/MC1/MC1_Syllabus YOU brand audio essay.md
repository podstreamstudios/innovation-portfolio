## Course Description

This microcredential examines the construction of persona as a cultural object, with particular attention to voice as a medium of authority and authenticity. Students develop and produce an audio essay that functions as a spoken extension of a personal brand concept (YOU®), exploring how identity, performance, and mediation operate through sound.

**NEW**: This updated version includes analysis of professional multi-platform personal branding through the case study of podcaster/news anchor Chris Hayes, examining how voice-based media evolved from enhanced podcasts (2005-2007) to contemporary platform ecosystems.

**Podcasting is framed explicitly as an emergent audio form**—not as a mature platformed medium—positioning the work at the threshold before platform-mediated identity, algorithmic distribution, and parasocial audio publics became infrastructural.

---

## Historical Context: The Time Capsule Frame

This microcredential is derived from a course taught 2002-2006 at SFU's School of Interactive Arts & Technology, **frozen at 2006** just before three fundamental ruptures restructured cultural production:

1. **Podcasting** → Platform-mediated identity (before voice became ambient/infrastructural)
2. **Social media** → Ubiquitous algorithmic mediation (before feed logic replaced human navigation)
3. **Artificial Intelligence** → Synthetic/generative media (before generative production without human authorship)

**This positioning is intentional**. By working within pre-platform logic, you'll experience what changed when these technologies emerged, enabling reflexive analysis of contemporary cultural conditions.

### The 2007 iPhone Discontinuity

**June 2007** marked a fundamental rupture in how podcasts were created and consumed:

**Before (2005-2007): Enhanced Podcasts**
- Downloaded to iPod (not phone), synced via iTunes
- Included embedded images, chapter markers, clickable links
- Bounded experiences (finite episodes, clear beginning/end)
- Minimal monetization (hobbyist/public radio model)
- Offline-first design

**After (2007+): Platform Streaming**
- Consumed on smartphones (always accessible)
- Visual elements separate (YouTube video podcasts OR audio-only)
- Infinite scroll/autoplay queues
- Ad-supported or platform-exclusive deals
- Streaming-dependent

**You'll work in the "before" paradigm** to understand what changed, then analyze contemporary podcasters who navigate the "after" ecosystem.

### 2. Platform Persona Analysis: Chris Hayes Case Study **[NEW]**

**Specifications**:
- **Length**: 4-5 pages
- **Format**: PDF or Word document

**Required Analysis**:

**Part A: Multi-Platform Persona Matrix** (2-3 pages)

Analyze Chris Hayes (or approved alternative) across platforms:

| Platform | Primary Affordance | Persona Version | Audience | Monetization |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|----------|--------------|
| MSNBC TV | Visual, regulated, 28min | Authoritative anchor | Cable news viewers | Network ads |
| Podcast | Audio, long-form, intimate | Curious intellectual | Engaged listeners | Dynamic ads |
| Book | Written, permanent, solo | Essayist/scholar | Readers | Publisher sales |
| Live Event | Physical, synchronous | Public intellectual | Cultural elite | Tickets + recording |

**Required Components**:
1. **Platform-specific affordances**: What does each medium enable/constrain?
2. **Persona adaptation**: How does Hayes's performance change across platforms?
3. **Brand coherence**: What remains consistent? What varies? Why?
4. **Audience segmentation**: Same people or different audiences on different platforms?

**Part B: Enhanced Podcast History** (1-2 pages)

Compare pre-iPhone enhanced podcasts (2005-2007) to current streaming ecosystem:

**What We Lost**:
- Boundedness (finite, completable episodes)
- Embedded visuals (chapter markers, synchronized images)
- Offline-first design
- Non-commercial hobbyist culture

**What We Gained**:
- Accessibility (phone always with you)
- Discoverability (algorithmic recommendations)
- Video options (YouTube podcasts)
- Creator monetization (sustainable careers)

**Analysis**: If you launched podcast today, what would you preserve from enhanced podcast era? What would you adopt from current ecosystem?

**Weight**: 25% of final grade

## Explicit Temporal Framing

**This microcredential treats podcasting as an emergent audio form**, not as a mature political or platformed medium. 

**Students should NOT model their work on contemporary podcast genres** that presume:
- Audience capture and growth
- Algorithmic recommendation and discovery
- Branding funnels and monetization
- Platform metrics (followers, downloads, likes)

**Instead, draw inspiration from**:
- Radio essays (NPR, BBC, CBC)
- Audio documentaries
- Public lectures and recorded talks
- Spoken-word criticism
- Enhanced podcasts (2005-2007 with chapter markers, embedded images)

**This constraint is intentional and methodological**. It allows you to experience podcasting at its **point of emergence**—before it consolidated into platform-driven, personality-centric infrastructure.

**However**, through the Hayes analysis, you'll **examine** contemporary platform podcasting to understand what changed—making visible the transformation from bounded, enhanced podcasts to infinite streaming ecosystems.

## Assignment Details: Platform Persona Analysis

### Research Phase (Suggested 2-3 hours)

**Required Viewing/Listening**:
1. Watch 20-30 minutes of Chris Hayes on MSNBC (YouTube clips acceptable)
2. Listen to full Hayes/Haidt podcast episode (60-90 min) - transcript provided
3. Browse Hayes's social media OR read book excerpt OR watch Hayes as guest on another podcast

**Optional for Deeper Analysis**:
- Watch Hayes's earlier MSNBC show "Up with Chris Hayes" (weekend morning, different format)
- Listen to multiple "Why Is This Happening?" episodes (see range of topics/guests)
- Read Hayes essays from The Nation (early print journalism)

### Writing Phase (Suggested 3-4 hours)

**Part A: Platform Matrix** (2-3 pages)
- Create detailed comparison table
- Analyze each platform's affordances
- Identify persona variations with specific examples
- Discuss brand coherence (what's consistent, what varies)

**Part B: Enhanced Podcast History** (1-2 pages)
- Research enhanced podcast features (2005-2007)
- Compare to current streaming ecosystem
- "What We Lost, What We Gained" analysis
- Personal strategy: What would YOU preserve/adopt?

### Guiding Questions

**For Platform Analysis**:
- How does Hayes's voice literally change across platforms? (Tone, pacing, energy)
- What topics work for podcast that wouldn't work for TV? Vice versa?
- Does Hayes curse in podcast? Would he on TV? Why/why not?
- The Strand event was at independent bookstore, not Barnes & Noble. What does venue signal?
- Is Hayes's podcast episode conversation marketing (for book) or content? Both?

**For Enhanced Podcast History**:
- If you could time-travel to 2006 and launch podcast, would you use chapter markers? Embedded images?
- What's lost with death of enhanced podcasts? (Boundedness, multimodality, offline-first)
- What's gained with streaming? (Accessibility, discoverability, monetization)
- Would you make podcast platform-exclusive (like Rogan/Spotify) or multi-platform distributed?

## Post-Assignment Reflexive Prompt

After completing YOUR audio essay and Hayes analysis, consider:

### Reflexive Questions (Not Graded, But Encouraged)

1. **Platform Pressure**: How would YOUR audio essay change if you were producing for Spotify with expectation of audience growth?
   - Would you optimize for algorithmic discovery?
   - Would you shorten/lengthen? Add hooks? Change topics?

2. **Monetization Impact**: If YOUR podcast needed to earn money, how would that shape content?
   - Ad breaks (interruption)
   - Sponsor messages (editorial independence)
   - Patreon tiers (exclusivity, premium content)
   - Platform exclusivity deals (access restriction)

3. **Synthetic Voice**: If YOU had access to AI voice generation, would you still use your own voice?
   - What's lost with synthetic voice? (Authenticity, labor, humanity)
   - What's gained? (Perfection, editability, scalability)
   - Where's the line between enhancement and replacement?

4. **Boundlessness**: Your audio essay is 8-12 minutes (bounded). Current podcasts often 60-180 minutes.
   - Is longer better? Or does boundedness serve listener?
   - Does finite length force tighter arguments?
   - Is "bingeable" content preferable to discrete episodes?

5. **Multi-Platform Identity**: Hayes is different on TV vs. podcast vs. book.
   - Is this authentic adaptation or inauthentic performance?
   - Are YOU different on different platforms? (Instagram vs. LinkedIn vs. dating apps)
   - Is coherent cross-platform identity possible? Desirable?

**These questions invite you to contrast YOUR pre-platform experience with contemporary podcast culture**—making visible what changed when voice became platformized, algorithmically distributed, and monetized.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### About the Hayes Analysis

**Q: Can I analyze a different podcaster instead of Chris Hayes?**  
A: Yes, with instructor approval. Must be someone with multi-platform presence (TV/podcast/book/social OR podcast/YouTube/Patreon/live events). Examples: Ezra Klein, Hasan Piker, Contrapoints, Philosophy Tube. Get approval before starting research.

**Q: Do I have to watch/listen to all four Hayes platforms?**  
A: Minimum two (TV + podcast recommended). Analyzing all four makes for richer analysis, but not required.

**Q: I don't have an iPod from 2005. How do I experience enhanced podcasts?**  
A: Use Internet Archive examples, read documentation, watch YouTube demos of features. You're analyzing historically, not replicating the exact experience.

### About the Audio Essay

**Q: Can I change my audio essay after doing the Hayes analysis?**  
A: Depends on timeline. Ideally, Hayes analysis (Module 2-3) comes before full audio essay production (Module 4-5) to inform your choices. But if audio essay is already done, reflection should address what you'd change if you could start over.

**Q: What if my audio essay doesn't fit any platform?**  
A: That's fine and potentially interesting! Reflect on whether you were avoiding platform thinking (artistic purity) or hadn't considered it yet. Both are valid.

**Q: Can I make a video podcast instead of audio-only?**  
A: No. This assignment specifically focuses on voice and audio. The constraint is methodological—experiencing audio-only forces different creative decisions than video. In reflection, you can discuss what video version would add/change.

### About the Enhanced Podcast History

**Q: Why does enhanced podcast history matter if that technology is dead?**  
A: Understanding what we lost helps us see what current platforms prioritize (streaming, engagement, monetization) vs. what earlier designs valued (boundedness, multimodality, offline access). Historical perspective makes contemporary choices visible as choices, not inevitabilities.

**Q: Would chapter markers work with my audio essay?**  
A: Potentially! If your essay has clear sections, chapter markers would help listeners navigate. This is a feature Soundtrap doesn't support, but you could note where you would place them in your reflection.
