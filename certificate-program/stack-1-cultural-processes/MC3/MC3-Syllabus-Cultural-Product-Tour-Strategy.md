## Course Description

This microcredential applies business strategy tools (Strategyzer methodology) to cultural production, teaching students to design sustainable tour models for promoting cultural products. Building on MC1 (branded persona creation via Soundtrap) and MC2 (experiential event design via ARIES), students now learn to coordinate multi-city tours that create value across stakeholder networks.

**The course focuses on LOGISTICS and VALUE CREATION**, not on creative content production. Students learn:
- Business Model Canvas (understanding stakeholder value flows)
- Value Proposition Canvas (designing offerings that meet customer needs)
- Lean Canvas (validating assumptions before committing resources)
- Tour coordination (multi-city logistics, partnerships, scaling)
- Platform strategy (coordinating presence across channels)

**For this microcredential, assume your MC1 cultural product has achieved modest success:**

- 5,000-10,000 regular listeners (niche but dedicated)
- Strong engagement (reviews, social shares, word-of-mouth)
- A book deal offer based on podcast's themes ($15K-25K advance)

**If MC1 was audio memoir/essay:**
- Interest from literary agents or small publishers
- Inclusion in audio anthology or curated collection
- Invitation to present at literary festivals

**If MC1 was experimental audio art:**
- Gallery exhibition opportunities
- Sound art festival invitations
- Commissioning offers from cultural institutions

**If MC1 was educational audio series:**
- Institutional partnerships (museums, universities, cultural centers)
- Workshop/masterclass opportunities
- Curriculum integration possibilities

**Regardless of MC1 format, the premise is:**
"Your audio work resonates enough that there's demand for live/touring presentations. Now you must strategize how to coordinate a tour that creates value for all stakeholders while sustaining economically."

### What Product Are You Touring?

**Students choose and defend what cultural product their tour promotes:**

**Option A: Book (based on MC1 themes)**
- Tour = book promotion circuit (readings, signings, conversations)
- MC1 audio = proof of concept that became book
- Venues: Bookstores, libraries, literary centers

**Option B: Podcast Live (MC1 audio as ongoing series)**
- Tour = live podcast recordings with local guests
- MC1 episode = pilot that became series
- Venues: Theaters, cultural spaces, hotel ballrooms (MC2 skills)

**Option C: Audio Installation/Performance (MC1 as art piece)**
- Tour = gallery/museum circuit with audio exhibits
- MC1 work = prototype for touring installation
- Venues: Galleries, museums, universities, sound art festivals

**Option D: Workshop/Masterclass Series (MC1 as teaching tool)**
- Tour = educational workshops on audio production, storytelling, or MC1 theme
- MC1 work = exemplar you teach from
- Venues: Universities, community colleges, cultural organizations, libraries

**Option E: Hybrid (combination)**
- Tour includes multiple formats (reading + workshop, podcast + signing, etc.)
- MC1 work = anchor but tour offers multiple value propositions
- Venues: Mix of spaces depending on city/format

**Key point:** What matters is the LOGISTICS, VALUE CREATION, and STRATEGY—not which product type you choose. You must defend your choice (why this product? Why tour format?) but focus is on coordination, not content creation.

### Week 2: Business Model Canvas (Stakeholder Analysis)

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Deep dive: Business Model Canvas (BMC) 9 blocks
- Cultural production context: How BMC applies to tours (not just startups)
- Stakeholder analysis: Who creates/receives value in a tour?
- Example BMC analysis: Chris Hayes live podcast tour (or similar cultural tour)

**The 9 Blocks of Business Model Canvas:**

**1. Customer Segments:**
Who are you creating value for?
- Your MC1 audience (podcast listeners, audio essay fans, etc.)
- Book readers (if applicable)
- Venue regulars (bookstore customers, gallery attendees, university communities)
- Curiosity seekers (people who discover you through tour promotion)

**2. Value Propositions:**
What value do you deliver to each segment?
- For audience: Live experience, meet creator, participate, get signature, connect with community
- For venues: Compelling programming, draw audience, cultural credibility
- For sponsors: Access to niche audience, brand alignment, activation opportunities

**3. Channels:**
How do you reach customer segments?
- Discovery: Podcast platforms, social media, venue networks, local press
- Sales: Eventbrite, venue box office, direct email
- Delivery: Physical venues (bookstores, ballrooms, galleries, universities)
- After-sales: Podcast episodes (tour recordings), social media follow-up

**4. Customer Relationships:**
What relationships do you establish with each segment?
- Personal assistance (meet & greet, Q&A, signing)
- Self-service (buy tickets online, find info on website)
- Communities (facilitate fan connections, create ongoing community)
- Co-creation (audience participation in recordings, workshops)

**5. Revenue Streams:**
What are customers willing to pay for?
- Ticket sales (primary—$15-35 per event)
- Product sales (books, merch, recordings at events)
- Sponsorships (brand partnerships)
- Workshop fees (if educational format)
- Institutional fees (university speaking fees, gallery commissions)

**6. Key Resources:**
What assets are essential?
- Your brand/reputation (built from MC1 work)
- MC2 event design skills (create better experiences than typical tours)
- Tour budget (book advance, savings, sponsor commitments)
- Production equipment (recording gear, AV setup)
- Content library (MC1 work, additional episodes/chapters, portfolio)

**7. Key Activities:**
What key things must you do?
- Live presentations/performances/recordings (core offering)
- Content creation (social media, behind-scenes, promotional materials)
- Logistics coordination (travel, venue booking, tech setup)
- Marketing/promotion (local press, social campaigns, venue partnerships)
- Relationship management (venues, sponsors, guests, audience)

**8. Key Partnerships:**
Who are your key partners/suppliers?
- Venues (bookstores, cultural spaces, hotels, galleries, universities)
- Sponsors (brands aligned with your theme)
- Guests (local experts, co-presenters, interviewees)
- Distribution platforms (podcast hosts, publishers, social media)
- Service providers (travel, equipment rental, marketing agencies if applicable)

**9. Cost Structure:**
What are the key costs?
- **Fixed per city:** Venue rental, travel, equipment, marketing
- **Variable per city:** Catering (if included), staffing, printed materials
- **One-time:** Tour development, promotional materials, website/Eventbrite setup
- **Ongoing:** Platform subscriptions, insurance, administrative overhead

**Asynchronous Work**:

**Draft YOUR Business Model Canvas:**

For each of the 9 blocks, document YOUR tour:
- Customer Segments: Who is your audience? (Based on MC1 persona + hypothetical growth)
- Value Propositions: What do they get from attending live? Why come vs. just listen/read?
- Channels: How do you reach them? (Email list? Social? Venue networks? Local media?)
- Customer Relationships: How do you interact before/during/after tour?
- Revenue Streams: What are realistic revenue sources? (Tickets? Product sales? Sponsors? Fees?)
- Key Resources: What do you have/need? (Skills, budget, equipment, brand)
- Key Activities: What must you do consistently? (Perform, coordinate, market, create content)
- Key Partnerships: Who do you need? (Venues, sponsors, guests, platforms)
- Cost Structure: What does it cost? (Travel, venues, marketing, production)

**Format:** Use Strategyzer tool, Miro template, or Google Slides with BMC layout

**Deliverable:** Business Model Canvas Draft (1-page visual + 2-3 pages explanatory notes)

### Week 3: Value Proposition Canvas (Customer-Value Fit)

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Deep dive: Value Proposition Canvas (VPC)
- Understanding customer jobs, pains, gains (not assumptions—research)
- Designing value propositions that fit customer needs
- Testing fit: Does your offering actually address customer pains/gains?

**The Value Proposition Canvas (Two Sides):**

**RIGHT SIDE: Customer Profile**

**Customer Jobs:**
What are customers trying to get done?
- Functional: Consume interesting content, learn something, be entertained
- Social: Signal identity ("I'm into smart/niche culture"), connect with like-minded people
- Emotional: Feel inspired, feel less alone, feel part of something meaningful

**Customer Pains:**
What annoys customers before/during/after trying to get job done?
- Podcast/audio is disembodied (can't see/meet creator, feels one-way)
- Hard to find community (fellow fans invisible, feel isolated in niche interests)
- Passive consumption (want to participate, not just consume)
- FOMO if tour doesn't come to their city
- Expensive to travel to major cities if they're in smaller markets
- Risk of disappointing experience (will live event match expectations?)

**Customer Gains:**
What outcomes/benefits do customers want?
- Memorable experience (story to tell, photos to share)
- Meet creator (put face to voice, have conversation, get signature)
- Connect with community (meet other fans, feel less alone)
- Exclusive content (live event different from regular podcast/book)
- Deepen engagement (strengthen connection to creator's work)
- Physical keepsake (signed book, photo, tangible memory)

**LEFT SIDE: Value Map**

**Products & Services:**
What are you offering?
- Live presentation/reading/recording (60-90 min)
- Q&A session (audience participation)
- Meet & greet / signing (personal interaction)
- Social time (pre/post event mingling, facilitated connections)
- Behind-scenes content (tour diary, social media exclusives)

**Pain Relievers:**
How does your offering alleviate pains?
- Brings experience to their city (no FOMO, no expensive travel)
- Creates embodied encounter (not disembodied, see/hear/meet in person)
- Facilitates community formation (fans meet each other, exchange contacts)
- Enables participation (ask questions, be in recording, contribute)
- Delivers quality experience (MC2 event design skills ensure it's good)

**Gain Creators:**
How does your offering create gains?
- Premium experience (designed for memorability, not just functional)
- Authentic interaction (real conversation, not performative)
- Community ritual (shared experience, inside jokes, ongoing connection)
- Tangible keepsake (signed book/poster, photo with creator, merch)
- Content participation (your voice on episode, your question featured)
- Gateway to ongoing engagement (deepen relationship with work)

**Testing Fit:**
- Do your Pain Relievers actually address Customer Pains?
- Do your Gain Creators actually create Customer Gains?
- Are you assuming or do you have evidence? (This is key—validate, don't guess)

**Asynchronous Work**:

**Draft YOUR Value Proposition Canvas:**

**Customer Profile (Right Side):**
- List 3-5 Customer Jobs (what are attendees trying to accomplish?)
- List 3-5 Customer Pains (what frustrates them about current content consumption?)
- List 3-5 Customer Gains (what would make experience worthwhile?)

**Value Map (Left Side):**
- List your Products & Services (what exactly happens at tour event?)
- List Pain Relievers (how does your event address each pain?)
- List Gain Creators (how does your event create each gain?)

**Test Fit:**
- For each Pain, do you have a Pain Reliever? (If not, add one or acknowledge gap)
- For each Gain, do you have a Gain Creator? (If not, add one or acknowledge gap)
- Are you making assumptions or do you have evidence? (Survey fans? Look at comparable tours?)

**Format:** Use Strategyzer tool, Miro template, or Google Slides with VPC layout

**Deliverable:** Value Proposition Canvas Draft (1-page visual + 2-3 pages explanatory notes + validation evidence)

### Week 4: Lean Canvas + City Selection

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Deep dive: Lean Canvas (lean startup methodology for cultural production)
- Validating assumptions before committing resources (don't build tour nobody wants)
- Metrics that matter (what numbers tell you if tour is working?)
- City selection criteria (where should you tour?)

**The Lean Canvas (9 Boxes, Different from BMC):**

**1. Problem:**
What problem are you solving?
- Audience wants deeper engagement than passive listening/reading
- Venues/institutions need compelling cultural programming
- You need to promote cultural product (book, podcast, installation, etc.)

**2. Customer Segments:**
Who has the problem?
- Your MC1 audience in touring cities
- Venue attendees (bookstore regulars, gallery-goers, university students)
- Cultural organizations seeking programming

**3. Unique Value Proposition:**
Single clear message: Why you? Why now?
- "Experience [your MC1 work] live—meet the creator, join the conversation, connect with community"
- OR "Workshop: Learn [MC1 skill/topic] from creator of [MC1 work]"
- OR "[MC1 audio installation] comes to your city for immersive gallery experience"

**4. Solution:**
Top 3 features solving problem:
- Live presentation/performance (embodies disembodied content)
- Community facilitation (connects isolated fans)
- Participation opportunity (active vs. passive)

**5. Channels:**
Path to customers:
- Owned: MC1 platform (podcast/audio), email list, social media
- Earned: Venue promotion, local press, cross-promotion with similar creators
- Paid: Facebook/Instagram ads, local event listings

**6. Revenue Streams:**
- Ticket sales (primary)
- Product sales (books, merch)
- Sponsorships (if applicable)
- Institutional fees (universities, museums)

**7. Cost Structure:**
- Fixed: Travel, accommodation, equipment, marketing
- Variable: Venue rental, catering, staffing, materials

**8. Key Metrics:**
What numbers matter?
- Ticket sales per city (goal: 80-120 attendees)
- Product sales conversion (goal: 50-70% buy book/merch)
- Email list growth (capture attendees for future)
- Social media engagement (shares, mentions, clips)
- Audience growth (new podcast subscribers/readers post-tour)

**9. Unfair Advantage:**
What can't be easily copied?
- You have existing audience (built-in demand)
- You have MC2 event design skills (better experiences than typical tours)
- You understand multi-platform strategy (MC1 Chris Hayes analysis)

**Asynchronous Work**:

**Task 1: Complete Lean Canvas**
Draft all 9 boxes for YOUR tour

**Task 2: City Selection (5-7 cities)**

**Criteria for selecting cities:**

**1. Audience concentration:**
- Where does your MC1 audience live? (Even if hypothetical, estimate based on comparable creators)
- Minimum threshold: 200-500 potential attendees per city (to ensure 80-120 ticket sales at 30-50% conversion)

**2. Venue availability:**
- **For book tour:** Independent bookstores, libraries, literary centers
- **For podcast tour:** Cultural spaces, university auditoriums, hotel ballrooms (MC2 design skills)
- **For audio installation:** Galleries, museums, sound art spaces
- **For workshops:** Universities, community colleges, cultural organizations

**3. Travel logistics:**
- Can cities be clustered? (East Coast: NYC → Boston → DC → Philly)
- Transportation efficient? (Train corridors better than cross-country flights)
- Tour duration realistic? (6-8 weeks for 5-7 cities)

**4. Market characteristics:**
- Cultural scene strength (bookstores, universities, arts organizations)
- Demographics match your audience (age, education, interests)
- Local media opportunities (community radio, alt-weeklies, blogs)

**5. Partnership potential:**
- Venues willing to co-promote? (Bookstores with email lists, universities with student orgs)
- Local sponsors possible? (Coffee shops, breweries, cultural orgs)
- Local guests available? (Experts, artists, community members to feature)

**Examples of city tiers:**

**Tier 1: Must-Do (large audience, strong infrastructure):**
- New York, Los Angeles, San Francisco (major cultural centers)

**Tier 2: Strong Secondary Markets:**
- Portland, Austin, Chicago, Seattle, Boston, DC (strong indie/cultural scenes)

**Tier 3: Strategic Add-Ons:**
- Smaller cities with niche fit (e.g., if MC1 about food: New Orleans, Montreal)
- University towns (if workshop format: Ann Arbor, Chapel Hill, Boulder)
- Regional hubs (if reaching underserved markets: Omaha, Boise, Albuquerque)

**For each city, document:**
- City name + specific venue (e.g., "Portland - Powell's Books")
- Audience estimate (realistic # of potential attendees)
- Venue details (capacity, rental cost if applicable, AV capabilities)
- Travel logistics (distance from previous city, transportation method)
- Unique opportunities (local partnerships, media, guests)

**Deliverable:** Lean Canvas (1-page) + City Selection Document (5-7 cities, 2-3 pages total with justifications)

### Week 5: Tour Logistics Design

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Multi-city coordination challenges
- Timeline planning (tour calendar, booking lead times, promotion schedules)
- Budget modeling across multiple cities
- Risk assessment and contingency planning

**Asynchronous Work**:

**Task 1: Master Tour Timeline**

Create calendar showing:
- **Planning phase:** 3-6 months before tour starts
  - Venue outreach and booking (4-6 months ahead)
  - Guest confirmations (3-4 months ahead)
  - Marketing materials creation (2-3 months ahead)
  - Ticket sales launch (6-8 weeks ahead)
- **Tour execution:** Week-by-week schedule
  - City 1: [Date range], travel days, event day, rest/travel day
  - City 2: [Date range], etc.
- **Post-tour:** Content release schedule
  - Edit recordings (if podcast tour)
  - Social media follow-up (photos, clips, thank-yous)
  - Audience survey (what worked? What to improve?)

**Task 2: Per-City Budget Model**

For EACH city in your tour, estimate:

**Costs:**
- Travel: Flight/train + local transport = $300-800
- Accommodation: Hotel 1-2 nights = $150-300
- Venue rental: $0-500 (bookstores often free, ballrooms/galleries charge)
- Equipment rental: AV, recording gear = $100-300 (if not venue-provided)
- Marketing: Local ads, printed materials = $100-300
- Catering: Light refreshments = $50-200 (if included)
- Staffing: Event support, if needed = $0-200
- **Total per city:** $700-2,600 (varies widely based on choices)

**Revenue:**
- Ticket sales: [# attendees] × $[price] = $[total]
  - Conservative: 60 × $20 = $1,200
  - Moderate: 90 × $25 = $2,250
  - Optimistic: 120 × $30 = $3,600
- Product sales: [# sold] × $[your cut] = $[total]
  - If book tour: 50 books × $8 = $400
  - If merch: 30 items × $10 = $300
- Sponsorship: $0-1,000 per city (if secured)
- **Total per city:** $1,600-4,900

**Net per city:**
- Conservative scenario: $1,600 revenue - $2,600 costs = **-$1,000 loss**
- Moderate scenario: $2,650 revenue - $1,800 costs = **+$850 profit**
- Optimistic scenario: $4,900 revenue - $1,200 costs = **+$3,700 profit**

**Task 3: Total Tour Budget**

Aggregate across all cities:
- Total costs (all cities combined)
- Total revenue (all scenarios)
- Break-even analysis (minimum tickets needed)
- Funding strategy (book advance covers tour? Need sponsors? Self-funded?)

**Task 4: Risk Assessment**

**What could go wrong?**
- Ticket sales lower than projected (50% of expected)
- Sponsor falls through (lose $1,000/city revenue)
- Venue cancels last minute (need backup plan)
- Travel delays (miss event, need contingency)
- Equipment failure (recording doesn't work, no backup)
- Personal emergency (illness, family crisis—can tour continue?)

**For each risk, document:**
- Likelihood (low/medium/high)
- Impact (minor/moderate/severe)
- Mitigation strategy (how to prevent or respond)

**Task 5: Partnership Strategy**

**Who do you need to make tour work?**

**Venues:**
- What's your pitch? (Why should they host you?)
- What's in it for them? (Audience, programming, revenue share?)
- How do you approach? (Cold email template, mutual connections, persistence)

**Sponsors:**
- Who would sponsor? (Brands aligned with MC1 theme)
- What do they get? (Logo on materials, product placement, speaking opportunity)
- How much can you ask? ($500? $2,000? $5,000? Based on audience size)

**Guests:**
- Who are ideal guests in each city? (Local experts, artists, community leaders)
- What's value exchange? (They get promotion, you get local credibility and draw)

**Media:**
- Local press: Community newspapers, alternative weeklies, blogs
- Podcasts: Guest on local podcasts to cross-promote
- University/community radio: Live interviews, event announcements

**Deliverable:** Tour Logistics Document (10-15 pages)
- Master timeline (Gantt chart or calendar view)
- Per-city budget models (spreadsheet)
- Total tour budget (3 scenarios: conservative/moderate/optimistic)
- Risk assessment matrix
- Partnership strategy (venue pitch, sponsor proposal, guest outreach, media plan)

### Week 6: Pilot Event Design (MC2 Integration)

**Synchronous Session** (2 hours):
- Applying MC2 ARIES methodology to tour stops
- Designing ONE city event in detail (proof of concept)
- Integrating Strategyzer strategy with MC2 experiential design
- Workshop: Teams work on pilot event, instructor consultation

**Asynchronous Work**:

**Task: Design ONE tour stop in complete detail**

Choose your STRONGEST city (best venue, largest audience, most partnerships)

Apply full MC2 ARIES methodology:

**ARIES Layer 1: Cultural Reservoir**
- Your MC1 cultural product (podcast episode, book chapter, audio piece)
- Your personal brand (YOU® from MC1)
- Local cultural context (what's unique about this city?)
- Guest/collaborator (local expert, artist, community figure)
- Venue identity (bookstore character, gallery mission, university culture)

**ARIES Layer 2: Experiential Assembly**

**Spatial Design:**
- Venue layout (stage, seating, signing area, mingling space)
- Guest flow (arrival → reception → event → signing → departure)
- MC2 skills applied: How do you transform generic bookstore back room or ballroom into compelling experience?

**Temporal Sequencing:**
- Event timeline (minute-by-minute)
  - 6:30 PM: Doors, mingling, music
  - 7:00 PM: Welcome, intro guest
  - 7:15 PM: Presentation/reading/recording (60-75 min)
  - 8:30 PM: Q&A (15 min)
  - 8:45 PM: Signing/meet & greet (30-45 min)
  - 9:30 PM: Event concludes

**Atmospheric Coordination:**
- Sound: Background music (arrival), presentation audio, ambiance
- Visual: Stage setup, signage, branding materials, lighting
- Social: Facilitated introductions, community-building moments
- Food/drink: Light refreshments (if budget allows), local specialties

**ARIES Layer 3: Value Objectification**

**Budget (this specific city):**
- All costs itemized (venue, travel, equipment, marketing, etc.)
- Revenue projections (tickets, products, sponsorships)
- Profit/loss analysis

**Licensing/Permissions:**
- Venue contract (rental agreement, insurance requirements)
- Recording permissions (if podcast tour—venue consent, audience release)
- Music licensing (if playing recorded music—SOCAN/ASCAP)
- Any other permits needed

**Feasibility:**
- Can this actually happen? (Venue available? Budget realistic? Timeline achievable?)
- What's backup plan if primary venue falls through?
