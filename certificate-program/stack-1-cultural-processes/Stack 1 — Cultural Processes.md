# Stack 1 — Cultural Processes as Methods

Stack 1 establishes the methodological foundation of the certificate.

Before students engage institutional systems (Stack 2)  
or design orchestrated ecosystems (Stack 3),  
they must understand how cultural artifacts are constructed, framed, and mediated.

Stack 1 centers Processes.

Not as abstract workflows — but as lived methodological action.

---

## From Consumption to Construction

Most students encounter media as consumers.

Stack 1 requires them to become producers.

If Stack 2 asks:
> Within what institutional systems does production occur?

Stack 1 asks:
> How are cultural artifacts constructed in the first place?

Students examine:

- Narrative framing
- Persona construction
- Media mediation
- Audio production
- Symbolic positioning
- Tool influence on perception

They move from interpretation to creation.

---

## Tools as Perceptual Frames

Stack 1 introduces a core insight that underpins the entire certificate:

Tools do not simply help us act.  
Tools shape what we perceive as possible.

Media platforms, audio editing environments, and narrative formats are not neutral instruments.

They are framing devices.

Students learn to recognize:

- How format influences meaning
- How persona shapes reception
- How framing organizes attention
- How tool choice implies value orientation

Methodological awareness begins here.

---

## Persona as Mediating Artifact

Stack 1 explores how individuals become symbolic anchors within cultural ecosystems.

Through comparative case studies (e.g., Chris Hayes vs Hunter S. Thompson), students analyze:

- Persona construction
- Institutional affiliation
- Voice authority
- Narrative positioning
- Cultural myth formation

Icons are treated not merely as celebrities, but as mediating artifacts that shape communities of practice.

This establishes the foundational link between WHAT (community), WHO (division of labor), and WHERE (institutional context) that later stacks expand.

---

## Process as Skill & Iteration

Students produce original audio work using structured tools (e.g., Soundtrap).

The emphasis is not on polish alone.

It is on:

- Iteration
- Editing discipline
- Framing intentionality
- Audience awareness
- Reflective refinement

Process literacy becomes embodied competence.

This is the rehearsal stage of orchestration.

---

## Comparative Case Study Logic

Stack 1 mirrors later stacks structurally:

- Compare/contrast practitioners
- Produce applied media artifact
- Reflect on framing implications
- Analyze tool influence

The goal is to develop critical fluency in media construction before confronting institutional complexity.

---

## The Stack 1 Arc

- **MC1 — Persona, Podcasting & Narrative Framing**
  - Compare/contrast journalist personas
  - Produce proto-podcast episode
  - Analyze framing techniques

- **MC2 — Visualization, Prefiguration & Design Framing**
  - Use visualization tools to model environments
  - Understand design as anticipatory process
  - Explore framing through spatial representation

- **MC3 — Cultural Product Tour Strategy**
  - Introduce Strategyzer tools
  - Map value propositions
  - Analyze distribution and monetization dynamics

Stack 1 ends with structured exposure to Strategyzer —  
but as exploratory instrument, not ecosystem governor.

---

## Completion of Stack 1

By the end of Stack 1, students can:

- Construct media artifacts intentionally
- Recognize framing effects
- Analyze persona as symbolic infrastructure
- Use tools with methodological awareness
- Map basic value propositions
- Reflect on how media shapes perception

Stack 1 builds methodological literacy.

Before institutional diagnosis.

Before ecosystem orchestration.

## Stack 1 in the Certificate Arc

Stack 1 — Processes (Skill & Framing)  
Stack 2 — Resources (Institutional Structure)

Stack 1 establishes that cultural production is never neutral.

It is always mediated.

It is always framed.

And it always operates within tools that shape what becomes visible.
