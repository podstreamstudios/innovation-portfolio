## Course Description

MC9 is the culminating performance of the Advanced Design Certificate.

Students publicly present and defend the AI-augmented design studio architecture (MC7) and licensing/transparency framework (MC8) through a shared-value boundary object: a fundraiser activation concept designed to operate within institutional constraints.

MC9 is not simply a pitch. It is a live orchestration test.

Students must demonstrate that their system can:

- Align Resources, Processes, and Values (RPV)
- Integrate AI responsibly
- Sustain governance clarity
- Absorb critique
- Preserve legitimacy under pressure

This is the moment where production, institutional diagnosis, and orchestration converge.

---

# The Boundary Object

The boundary object is an AI-augmented, sponsor-integrated, cause-aligned activation concept designed for institutional deployment (e.g., museum context).

It must:

- Generate shared value across stakeholders
- Respect licensing and IP boundaries
- Integrate AI within governance limits
- Produce measurable economic and reputational value
- Maintain transparency and public trust

It is a boundary object because it operates at the intersection of multiple activity systems:

- Cultural production
- Institutional governance
- Sponsor ecosystems
- Public audiences
- Regulatory frameworks

Its success depends on systemic coherence, not aesthetic persuasion.

# Pitch & Governance Dialogue Format

MC9 unfolds in two integrated stages.

---

## Part 1 — Formal Studio & Boundary Object Presentation (20 minutes)

Teams present:

1. Studio Identity & Governance Architecture (MC7)
2. Resource Architecture (Viability)
3. Process Architecture (Feasibility)
4. Value Framing & Stakeholder Alignment (Desirability)
5. Licensing & Transparency Layer (MC8)
6. Strategyzer Business Model articulation (BMC + VPC)
7. AI integration boundaries
8. Institutional fit

The presentation must demonstrate:

- Clear division of labour
- Explicit governance logic
- Financial realism
- Transparency safeguards
- Compound-lens alignment

This is not speculative storytelling.

It is systemic articulation.

---

## Part 2 — Reviewer Dialogue & System Response (20 minutes)

Faculty and invited reviewers engage the team through critique and questioning.

Questions may address:

- Institutional alignment
- Financial vulnerability
- Sponsorship dependence
- Licensing complexity
- AI governance risk
- Public trust concerns
- Operational feasibility
- Ethical positioning

Students must respond in real time.

The evaluation focus is not rhetorical speed.

It is systemic absorption.

Teams must demonstrate:

- Where authority resides
- How critique is processed
- What layer adapts
- What remains stable
- How legitimacy is preserved

This is governance under pressure.

# Evaluation Framework

MC9 evaluates systemic performance across five dimensions:

---

## 1. Structural Coherence (RPV Alignment)

Do Resources, Processes, and Values reinforce one another?

Or are they loosely connected components?

---

## 2. Orchestration Clarity

Is division of labour explicit?

Are human and AI roles clearly differentiated?

Is governance structured?

---

## 3. Institutional Fit

Does the boundary object align with:

- Institutional mission
- Donor expectations
- Public education mandate
- Legal constraints
- Sponsor reputational logic

---

## 4. Licensing & Transparency Integrity

Are:

- IP rights clearly articulated?
- Revenue flows traceable?
- Sponsorship disclosures explicit?
- AI usage policies transparent?

Transparency is treated as system clarity, not overregulation.

---

## 5. Resilience Under Critique

When challenged:

- Does the system adapt?
- Does alignment hold?
- Does legitimacy remain credible?

This is the ultimate orchestration test.

# Autopoietic Test

The final evaluation question is simple:

If launched, will this studio reproduce itself?

Meaning:

- Do resources regenerate?
- Do processes stabilize?
- Do stakeholders re-engage?
- Does trust compound?
- Does legitimacy endure?

If yes, orchestration succeeds.

If not, it was only a presentation.
