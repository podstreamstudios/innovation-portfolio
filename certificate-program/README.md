## Overview

A nine-microcredential certificate program structured around the productive tension between cultural processes and institutional resources — and the shared values that can resolve that tension through AI-augmented systems design.

The certificate traces a dialectical arc: students first develop literacy in how culture is made and circulated (Stack 1), then how institutions resource and mediate that culture (Stack 2), and finally how to design AI-augmented systems that coordinate these two worlds through shared values (Stack 3). The program culminates in a live formative intervention capstone — a fundraising event designed as a boundary object between a cause-driven organization and a sponsor/donor ecosystem.

**Institutional pitch sentence:**

*Completion of the nine-microcredential certificate certifies foundational literacy in cultural production, institutional mediation, platform and workflow economics, multimodal experience design, and AI-augmented systems design — competencies that are typically assumed but not formally taught in senior undergraduate and graduate courses in arts, culture, creative industries, and design entrepreneurship.*

---

## Architecture

The certificate is organized as three stacks of three microcredentials each, structured as a dialectic:

### Stack 1 — Cultural Processes (MC1–MC3)
*Thesis. Local scale. Readiness for applied cultural analysis.*

Explores cultural production as it is lived and practiced — often in tension with the institutions that surround it. Develops individual voice, production literacy, platform economics, and comfort with case studies and abstraction.

**Prepares students for:** "What is arts & culture marketing?" / "Who is the artist?" / "Who is the consumer?"  
**Course alignment:** BUSI4229A (early weeks)

### Stack 2 — Institutional Resources (MC4–MC6)
*Antithesis. Global/institutional scale. Readiness for advanced mediation.*

Examines how institutions manage, fund, distribute, and mediate culture — often in tension with the organic needs of cultural producers. Covers archival interpretation, communities of practice, workflow and broadcasting economics.

**Prepares students for:** "The artist as brand" / "Distribution of arts & culture products" / "Cultural intermediaries" / "Experience design and management"  
**Course alignment:** BUSI4229A (later weeks)

### Stack 3 — Shared Values (MC7–MC9)
*Synthesis. Operational scale. Readiness for AI-augmented practice and live intervention.*

Attempts to resolve the tensions between Stacks 1 and 2 through AI-augmented systems design, IP and licensing strategy, and a live capstone intervention. Students design, build, and defend AI-augmented boundary objects that coordinate cultural and institutional systems through shared values.

**Capstone:** Students design and defend a fundraising event as a boundary object coordinating a cause-driven organization and a sponsor/donor ecosystem — including activity system modeling, Strategyzer-based ecosystem design, governance and IP structuring, a formal written proposal, a pitch deck, and a live stakeholder defense simulation.

## Capstone — Fundraiser Event as Formative Intervention & Boundary Object

The certificate culminates in a live formative intervention capstone centered on the design of a fundraising event and supporting digital infrastructure.

Students coordinate two distinct activity systems through a shared boundary object:
1. A cause-driven organization (e.g., a mental health charity or social initiative)
2. A sponsor/donor ecosystem with its own institutional logics and value structures

The event is not treated as "event planning" but as:
- A systems-level intervention
- A governance design challenge
- A shared value coordination mechanism
- A tertiary artifact that must be defended as intellectual property

Students model each activity system, identify structural contradictions, and design the event and digital infrastructure as a mediating artifact that aligns mission integrity, sponsor incentives, community trust, and economic sustainability.

**Final capstone deliverables:**
- Activity system modeling
- Strategyzer-based ecosystem design (used as second stimulus in a Change Laboratory framework)
- Governance and IP structuring
- A formal written proposal
- A pitch deck
- A live stakeholder defense simulation
- A portfolio of clients, sponsors, donors, and stakeholders assembled by the student

## Institutional Pitch

This program is designed to be:

- **Additive, not competitive** — it does not replace degree programs
- **Preparatory, not substitutive** — it feeds senior and graduate courses
- **Modular and portable** — each stack is independently licensable; case examples can be localized by institution

The dialectical structure (cultural processes → institutional resources → shared values) gives the certificate a theoretical spine grounded in Activity Theory, Change Laboratory methodology, and boundary object theory — frameworks that faculty in arts management, cultural policy, design, and creative industries will recognize and respect.

From an institutional perspective, this program:
1. **De-risks senior courses** — instructors no longer need to backfill foundational competencies
2. **Creates a recruiting funnel** — especially for interdisciplinary students in media, design, CS, and music
3. **Supports microcredential strategy without cannibalization** — the certificate feeds degree programs, not replaces them
